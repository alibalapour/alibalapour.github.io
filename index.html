Research Experiences

Research Assistant

Data Science and Machine Learning Lab, Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran
April 2022 - Present

In April 2022, I joined Multimedia Systems and Complex Networks in the Data Science and Machine Learning Lab as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Rabiee. One of our projects in the group is reviewing recent domain adaptation and domain generalization methods on functional brain data (like EEG signals, fMRI, etc.) and providing a survey paper in this field. My main role is reading and comprehending new papers in this area, gathering information about datasets and implementations, and preparing infrastructure to organize papers and data efficiently. Our work is undergoing, and we are expected to publish a high-quality paper soon.
In this project, I've learned about different approaches that can be used to solve the domain shift problem among distributions. For instance, domain adaptation or generalization can be achieved by using meta-learning and self-supervised learning (which are relatively hot topics in deep learning). Speaking about soft skills, I've gained experience in team-working, and I've learned that patience, punctuality, and discipline are the most important attributes a researcher should have.

Research Assistant

Robust and Interpretable Machine Learning Lab, Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran
September 2021 - Present

In September 2021, I joined the medical image analysis group in the Robust and Interpretable Machine Learning lab as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Rohban. I had two projects in the lab. The first was using novel self-supervised methods on pathology images and pre-training a powerful and compact pre-trained model to perform well on downstream tasks. The results are decent, but there is room for improvement. The SOTA model got 95.82% Macro-F1 score on NCT-CRC dataset, while we reached 95.04% Macro-F1 with much fewer parameters.
In the second project, I developed a model for recognizing acute lymphoblastic cells in blood smear images. Our model's performance was fine on ALL-IDB (a well-known dataset) and reached 90% ACC in zero-shot and 96% ACC in few-shot training.
We will publish the GitHub repository of both projects in the near future.
