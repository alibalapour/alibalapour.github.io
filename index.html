About Me

Hi! I'm Ali Balapour, a senior undergraduate student at the Sharif University of Technology. My main research interests are deep learning and its applications in medical imaging, bioinformatics, natural language processing, and interior design.
I want to broaden my knowledge and experience in mentioned areas by learning theoretical aspects and developing experimental sides of these methods in real-life problems. The majority of my experiences were on the practical side, and I enjoyed implementing and tuning DL and ML models. It's worth mentioning that I am an ML engineer in a tech company, and I have valuable experience in the ML industry.
My research goal is to propose innovative applications to solve current problems in computer vision and NLP, focusing on medical applications.

Research Experiences

Research Assistant

Data Science and Machine Learning Lab, Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran
April 2022 - Present

In April 2022, I joined Multimedia Systems and Complex Networks in the Data Science and Machine Learning Lab as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Rabiee One of our projects in the group is reviewing recent domain adaptation and domain generalization methods on functional brain data (like EEG signals, fMRI, etc.) and providing a survey paper in this field. My main role is reading and comprehending new papers, datasets, and implementations, in addition to preparing infrastructure to gather and organize papers and data efficiently. Our work is undergoing, and we are expected to publish a high-quality paper soon.

Research Assistant

Robust and Interpretable Machine Learning Lab, Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran
September 2021 - Present

In September 2021, I joined the medical image analysis group in the Robust and Interpretable Machine Learning lab as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Rohban I had two projects in the lab. The first was using novel self-supervised methods on pathology images and pre-training a powerful and compact pre-trained model to perform well on downstream tasks. The results are decent, but there is room for improvement.
In the second project, I developed a model for recognizing acute lymphoblastic cells in blood smear images. Our model's performance was fine on ALL-IDB (a well-known dataset) and reached 90% ACC in zero-shot and 96% ACC in few-shot training.
